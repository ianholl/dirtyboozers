Edgware Road - London

THE ROYAL EXCHANGE

Jimmy Boozers' Story

So I'm in Edgware Road about to meet up with some friends. Walk past a quality looking wee place on the corner - glass all steamed up. Got to scope this place out. Rude not to.

Historically and personally for me, I have often found this area around Marlybone and Paddington a little fancy (maybe I've just been hitting the wrong places) so I'm pleasantly surprised when I walk in. Firstly its quite lively for a Thursday night. Secondly there are horses everywhere - pictures and little models. And who doesn't love a horse yea?
  
As I shuffle to the bar, the staff are quick to note my thirsty appearance and rush to serve me. That's the sort of service you want. Standard DB.

Next thing I note is the male to female ratio is a solid 10 to 1. There are more horses than females in the pub.

There is sport on. 2 screens so multi-screen viewing. 1 has football on. The other, believe it or not, horse racing. You don't see that often. Horse racing on in the pub. Don't think it is encouraged. But this is an Irish pub and it seems to be quite popular amongst the regulars here.

The pub is tiny but has a quality charm about it and I immediately feel comfortable. Quality mix of people about. Not sure it would be ideal if a big game was on though. Would be a little tight. 

Fosters 4.60. Pricey. Didn't catch the Guiness price but probably same ball park.
2 Screens on the go for the sport.

There is a short food menu scribbled on a black board...
  - Sausage and Mash
  - Beef and Mash
  - Lamb and Mash
  Spuds all round - It is an Irish pub right enough. Quality.

Summary
Probably best boozer round this neck of the woods.
