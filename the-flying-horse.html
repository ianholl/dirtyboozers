The Flying Horse

Liverpool Street - London

The Flying Horse

Jimmy Boozers' Story

Liverpool vs Man Utd is happening right now. There's a fierce rivalry between the clubs and I'm excited. It's good for the neutral.

In the Liverpool street area. London City. Its a Sunday so most things are shut down but a beacon of light and hope radiates from the Flying Horse.

It's an old boozer in the heart of the capital. A boozer with a past. A boozer where they have clearly been forced to build a female toilet at some stage to meet regulation.

There is a hostel upstairs as well. Its only been open for a couple of years. Found out that that is the only reason why the pub is even open. The hostel gives it a good crowd. A mix of people from all over who are up for it.

Being a Sunday at this spot it is quietish. But for me it's the perfect volume of punters. You can get a seat and enjoy the game with a good bit of atmosphere.

In the bar I notice that a front window panel has been panned in. Its alright though because it's been gaffataped over. I find out some old geezer put his head through it yesterday during the day. Perhaps an overzealous celebration from the big man finishing his Christmas Shopping.

I'm sat enjoying the game when a couple of muppets walk in. Think this pub must be on Sesame Street or something. Big Bird and another monster creature lad. Cookie monster maybe. But this lads vice is much more serious than cookies.

£4.70 a lager (fosters).
£5.10 a Guinness.
Pricey.
You can get a £3 Ale. Deuchers. Scottish.

For the football it rarely gets better. 2 screens flank the projector either side. More screens fill the boozer making it harder to not watch the game than to watch it. Valhalla.

As my pint comes towards the end, much like the weekend, I'm asked "same again?". Rude not to.

Summary
Quality for the football but pricey. If your in the area then visit but don't travel. If you have a group of pals wanting to watch the big game it might be a good shout given the busyness.
